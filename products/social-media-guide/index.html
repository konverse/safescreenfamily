The Parent's Guide to Every Social Media Platform

Your kids are on platforms you’ve never heard of. And the ones you have heard of? They’ve changed.

Instagram isn’t just photos anymore. Discord isn’t just for gamers. TikTok’s DMs are wide open by default. And by the time a platform makes the news, your kid has been on it for months.

You can’t protect your child from what you don’t understand.

The Parent’s Guide to Every Social Media Platform gives you clear, honest, no-hype breakdowns of the 10 platforms your kids are most likely using – so you can have smarter conversations and set up real protection.

Platforms Covered

  1. TikTok – Short video, algorithm-driven feeds, DMs, live streaming, and what “FYP” actually means
  2. Instagram – Stories, Reels, Close Friends, vanish mode, and the features kids use that parents don’t see
  3. Snapchat – Disappearing messages, Snap Map location sharing, My Eyes Only, and why “it deletes” is a myth
  4. YouTube – Recommendations algorithm, YouTube Shorts, comments, and the difference between YouTube and YouTube Kids
  5. Discord – Servers, DMs, voice channels, screen sharing, and why it’s the new hangout spot
  6. BeReal – What it is, why teens love it, and the hidden social pressure behind “authenticity”
  7. Roblox – In-game chat, user-generated content, virtual currency, and predator risks parents miss
  8. Twitch – Live streaming culture, chat rooms, donations, and content your kid might be watching
  9. X (Twitter) – Trending topics, DMs, quote tweets, and exposure to adult content
  10. WhatsApp – Group chats, disappearing messages, and why your teen prefers it to texting

Each Platform Chapter Includes

  • How it works – explained in plain language, not tech jargon
  • What kids actually use it for – the real reasons, not the marketing pitch
  • Step-by-step privacy settings – with screenshots, so you can lock it down in minutes
  • Red flags to watch for – behavioral changes, language, and warning signs specific to that platform
  • Conversation starters – age-appropriate questions that open dialogue instead of shutting it down
  • Should my kid be on this? – Honest, age-based recommendations with nuance, not blanket bans

Why This Guide Is Different

This isn’t a scare piece. It’s not “social media is evil, delete everything.” It’s a practical, balanced guide written for parents who want to stay informed, set smart boundaries, and keep the lines of communication open.

Because the alternative – banning everything and hoping for the best – doesn’t work. Kids find workarounds. What works is understanding.
